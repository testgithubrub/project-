rules and regulation

    Promotion Rules

  • Promotion criteria for classes I to IX & XI stage will be governed by the Certificate Examination of the Board (CBSE).
  • A five point scale will be used to grade the achievement in Moral Science, Art-craft, Singing, P.T. and SUPW. Children should obtain at least a scale grade of D2 in each subject.
  • The School does not undertake to retain answer script for more than two months after the examinations.
  • Internal rechecks, totaling of the marks. Etc. will be completed by a scheme of standardization before reports are given out. Hence no answer scripts will be shown to parents.
  • Promotion to the next will not be granted until the final examination and compiling of the annual result.

    Rules for Withdrawal

  • Every application for Transfer Certificate shall be made in writing on the prescribed from obtainable from the Office by the Parents/Guardians.
  • A month’s written notice is required before a student can be withdrawn, In Lieu of such notice one installment of fee, in addition to the fee of the month in which the application for Transfer Certificate is made will be charged.
  • No Transfer Certificate will be granted unless all School dues have been cleared, satisfactorily arranged for and “NO DUES CERTIFICATE” is obtained from the fee counter, Librarian, Laboratory in charges P.T. Master.

    Attendance Rules

  • Every student is expected to be regular in attendance. He should be on time for the General Assembly. Repeated late comers be detained after School hours, charged a fine determined by the Principal, may be sent home, or may be asked to be withdrawn from the School.
  • No student leaves the School premises during the School hours without the written permission of the Principal.
  • No leave of absence for the whole day or part thereof is granted except for serious reasons, and only on previous written application to the Parents/Guardians.
  • A student who is absent during the term for more than three days on medical grounds is required to submit a medical certificates together with the leave application.
  • All leave applications should bear the signature of the Parents/Guardian and the class, section be clearly noted for speedy information to the respective class teacher

    Rules for Fees

  • As the CBSE Board has made "CASHLESS MODE" compulsory, you are requested to pay the fees through online payment, cheque, swipe machine (at school) with proper details of child/school blank account
  • All fee must be paid in Eight equal instalments in the months of April, July, August, September, October, November, December and January.
  • The last date of fees payment will be 20th of the above mentioned months. If the 20th is a Holiday or Sunday, fees will be collected on the following working day.
  • In case fees is not deposited by 20th, same can be deposited in the following month with Rs.100/- as fine per month. That means if fees is late for two months then you will be charged Rs.200/-as fine for that particular month.
  • Results of the Terminal and Final Examination may be withheld if the fees is not paid on time.
  • Results of the Terminal and Final Examination may be withheld if the fees is not paid on time.
  • Before The withdrawal of a pupil, a month's notice in writing or a month's fees in lieu of notice will be charged
  • For Classes IX- XII separate registration and examination fee will be charged as per the CBSE Board requirements.

    General Rules of Discipline

  • Students are urged to contribute to the tone of the school by their manners and general behavior.
  • They should be polite, friendly and well behaved in and outside the school. They should be respectful to their elders, teachers and truthful at all times.
  • They should be orderly, clean in their person, and neatly dressed and also co-operative to maintain cleanliness in the school.
  • As far as posture, cleanliness, silence, attention to work and respect to teachers and companions are concerned, a classroom should be looked upon as a place of reverence and discipline.
  • They should maintain absolute silence during the General Assembly and while moving into the classroom from General Assembly. Silence should also be observed on the way to and while returning from P.T., Library,Labs and Music Room.
  • The general assembly is the most important parts of the school day. Every students should be present to it, to being the day with prayer to God, to listen to notice, and talks of the Principal.
  • All must come to school neatly dresses with lessons well prepared and home-work done. Text and exercise books and material for drawing/craft should be brought to School according to the day's routine.
  • The Students should keep their own class room and the School compound spotlessly clean using the bins provided for waste paper and rubbish.

    Rules for School Library

  • The library is being progressively stocked with suitable and up-to date books to provide further healthy reading.
  • Children will be issued books for reading during their library periods, and the same must be returned before leaving the library.
  • Students may borrow one book at a time for a period upto one week from the library. In case the books are not returned on time, a fine of Rs.1 Per day will be charged. No books will be allowed to be taken home without the permission of the Librarian.
  • For books issued against a student’s name, full cost will be realized for the loss of the book, or in case of disfigurement, scratching scribbling, drawing or tearing the student may be asked to replace the book, or pay for damages.
